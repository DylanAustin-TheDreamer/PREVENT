Q: But what is a Radical Carrot?

Well, a radical carrot is a term used to describe an individual or group that has taken their beliefs or actions to an extreme level, often in a way that is harmful or disruptive to others. In the context of PREVENT, it refers to those who may pose a risk to the community through their radicalized views or behaviors.

Q: Well what's the difference between radical and radicalized?

The term "radical" refers to someone who holds extreme views or beliefs, while "radicalized" refers to the process by which an individual adopts these extreme views. Radicalization can occur through various means, such as exposure to extremist ideologies or social influences.

Q: What are the signs of radicalization?

Signs of radicalization can vary, but some common indicators include:

  • Increased isolation from friends and family

  • Adoption of extremist beliefs or ideologies

  • Changes in behavior, such as increased aggression or hostility

  • Engagement with extremist groups or individuals

  • Expressions of hatred or intolerance towards others
